The Old School Emulation Center
13/03/2025

Hello everyone - it's been a while, nearly a year between releases! We
hope you'll agree that the wait was worth it as we unveil yet another
bumper TOSEC release.

We'll start, as always, with the amazing work of our Commodore 64 and
Amiga teams. These systems are so popular and huge that they represent a
huge portion of the TOSEC catalogue all by themselves, and we're always
amazed at the amount of work that duncantwain, Crashdisk and others put
into not only adding the constant stream of new releases, but fixing and
properly cataloguing literally millions of files across hundreds of
DATs.

mictlantecuhtle continues in his quest to put his fingerprints on every
single DAT in the set, with contributions this time spanning across
nearly 600 DATs. This release again sees the addition of some wonderful
and obscure machines, covering all shapes and sizes, from the handheld
Casio PB-100 to the room-sized BCC 500 (which, while hosted at the
University of Hawaii, acted as the hub of the ALOHA system, a precursor
to the modern Ethernet). As well as many additions, this release also
sees the continuation of efforts to address some of TOSEC's legacy DATs,
most notably with the behemoth Tandy Radio Shack Model III and 4
"Various" DATs now split out into their separate types and extensions.

As always, thanks to those in our forums and Discord who have made their
own contributions - a particular shoutout here to smesgr for significant
contributions to the Atari 2600, GBA, PC and PSX DATs, but also in no
particular order to eientei, Pugsy, UnlockerPT, Wiggy2k, Cactus, star,
whatever000 and artifexor, all of whom have made contributions big or
small towards this release.

The ADF scanners have laid dormant in the TOSEC-PIX offices once again,
with members concentrating on adding more of the backlog. That being
said, the TOSEC-PIX update is the largest ever, weighing in at close to
750GB with 242 new and 20 updated dats!

At the TOSEC-ISO offices, things are still moving at a frenetic pace,
with PlayStation 2 WIP dats from 2009 added and also hundreds of
submissions added from members worldwide. More donations of physical
media have also been received from members to add to the project. Maddog
has been busy as usual with his .gdi Dreamcast contributions, but with
the excellent help from a new contributor, Captain Blood, they also
introduced a couple of brand new shiny .cdi dats. Since .cdi is the only
way to play using the original drive of the console, we felt that they
were long overdue to be added in the quest to preserve everything about
Dreamcast. Finally, this time we have some GameCube updates as well.
Looks like only the original XBox was left out of this console
generation, sorry big black box! We hope to correct this in the future.

A huge thanks to everyone who has donated either physical media or
submitted disc images to the project, it is most appreciated.

Be sure to join our Discord channel if you havenâ€™t already.

Enjoy this release and see you all in a few months!


************************************************************************

All folders and contents should be self-explanatory. All necessary
CUEs are also included. There are also some scripts included to assist
in creating TOSEC folders, and some 'move' scripts for those of you
using RomVault. As usual, be careful with long pathnames in some OSes.
Best advice is to use your folders as close as possible to the root of
the drive.

For all the latest work-in-progress news, as well as expanded details
on all upcoming changes and additions, keep up to date by visiting us
at: www.tosecdev.org

Enjoy!
